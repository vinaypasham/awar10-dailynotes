Network ACLs : 



Security Group: SG act as firewall at Instance level. SG are stateful.. We need to take care about the inbound rules. By default, We allow all traffic on outbound rules. 

NACL : Network Access Control List : Act as firewall at Subnet level. One subnet can contains multiple instances.
--> NACL is stateless, We need to open traffic InBound and outbound also. 
--> Traffic flow required ephemeral ports. 
--> NACL wont track taffic receiving and leaving ports. 
--> It uses Ephemeral ports (Temporary ports)

Inbound : Traffic coming into the subnet from outside. 
* Traffic coming from internet to connect your ec2 instance / access your webpage..
* Traffic from another subnet, entering into our subnet.
* Traffic from on-premise entering into subnet.

Outbound: Traffic leaving your subnet.. 
* Webserver running in our subnet is responding back to the client. 
* Ec2 instance, inside our subnet is accessing internet (google / ftp / fb). 
* Traffic going out to the outer world. 


Default Network ACL behaviour is "allows everything" in inbound and outbound.

VPC Peering: This feature helps to enable communication across VPCs.

Requester VPC and Accepter VPC can be in same region in same account.
Requester VPC and Accepter VPC can be in other region in same account.
Requester VPC and Accepter VPC can be in same region in account-1 and account-2.
Requester VPC and Accepter VPC can be in other region in account-1 and account-2.


If requester and accepter VPCs CIDR ranges are same, we cannot create VPC Peering. 
VPC Peering is non-transitive peering.
